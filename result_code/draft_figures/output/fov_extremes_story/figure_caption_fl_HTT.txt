METHODOLOGY:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Clustered mRNA per Cell calculation:
  - Cluster detection: DBSCAN algorithm (eps=0.75 µm, min_samples=3)
  - Intensity threshold: 2.5 × slide-specific peak intensity
  - Peak intensity: mode of KDE-fitted single spot intensity distribution
  - Clustered mRNA = Total cluster intensity / Peak intensity
  - Per-cell normalization: Clustered mRNA / N_nuclei in FOV

Extreme FOV definition:
  - Threshold = 95th percentile of Wildtype distribution (region-specific)
  - FOVs with Clustered mRNA/Cell > WT P95 classified as 'extreme'
  - This identifies Q111 FOVs in the tail of the distribution

Statistical tests:
  - Mann-Whitney U test: non-parametric comparison of medians
  - Tests whether Q111 and WT come from same distribution

Quality control:
  - Slides m1a2 and m1b5 excluded (technical failures)
  - Based on UBC positive control analysis

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The 'extreme FOV' analysis provides insight into the mosaic nature of the Q111

FOVs with dramatically elevated clustered mRNA. The breakdown by age, mouse, and
atlas coordinate helps identify:

  - Temporal dynamics: Does the extreme phenotype progress with age?
  - Individual variation: Are certain mice predisposed to extreme FOVs?
  - Spatial patterns: Are specific anatomical regions more affected?

This information is crucial for understanding whether the clustered mRNA phenotype
is driven by systemic factors (affecting all mice equally) or by stochastic/local
factors (creating mosaic patterns).
